About Me

Hi! I'm Jose Lima, a game developer studying at Information Systems, Computer Science, and Game Design at Carnegie Mellon University. Video games have been part of my life for as long as I can remember, and they're actually what I wrote my college essay about. That passion for video games is what pulled me towards how video games are built, from the low-level systems to real time graphics.


Education

At Carnegie Mellon, my coursework has centered on the systems and concepts underneath games and graphics. I have taken computer science courses involving systems, data structures, linear algebra, and graphics. This fall, I'm looking forward to 15-466, Computer Game Programming, which builds small games from nearly the ground up in C++ and OpenGL, each one focused on a specific runtime system like AI, physics, or rendering.


Extracurriculars

Beyond coursework, I have done research projects at the CMU Augmented Perception Lab where I've developed a VR Motion Capture Pipeline connecting Unity and OptiTrack (Motive), and a VR fitness trainer application. Additionally, I was part of the NASA SUITS 2026 Challenge, where I've worked on the frontend development for an AI voice assistant feature for astranaut spacesuit interfaces.


Outside of this, I'm the treasurer for the CMU's Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers, where I manage the organization's budget and help run event programming. I'm also part of the CMU's Game Creation Society, where I've worked on game projects in teams over the course of a semester, and I play for Magia Latina in CMU's intramural soccer league.
