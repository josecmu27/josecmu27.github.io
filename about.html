About Me

Hello! I'm Jose Lima, a software developer passionate about building engaging experineces via game development and software engineering. I am currently pursuing a degree in Information Systems at Carnegie Mellon University, and a minor in Computer Science and Game Design, where I have built my experience via course, club, and research projects. I chose this path because I want the ability to create anything I can imagine and provide people with engaging experiences via my work.


My journey started in high school, when a friend contacted me to join a Hackathon just to try it out. I asked him what where we going to build, and he said we could build anything we want and decided to build a video game. I have always been a gamer ever since I have memory and they have been an important part of my life as they have provided me with remarkable experiences and taught me to be adventurous. Therefore, I decided to join the Hackathon and I discovered a whole new world. Although it was a challenging experience and the final result was not what we expected, it made me want to learn how to build what I envisioned, and thus, my journey as a software developer started.


Education and Extra Curriculars

At Carnegie Mellon University, I’ve built a strong foundation in software development via coursework in Model-View-Controller design, Agile methodologies, Test-Driven Development, relational databases, and source control. Currently, I am collaborating with a team in my Mobile App Development course to design and implement a semester-long mobile application, applying these fundamentals in a real-world project setting.


Additionally, my computer science studies have also strengthened my problem-solving skills through work with fundamental data structures and algorithms. Currently, in my Computer Graphics course, I’m implementing core graphics functionalities such as a rasterizer and mesh editor in Scotty 3D, a C++ modeling and rendering package. Furthermore, I’ve also studied mathematics including linear algebra and calculus which has deepened my ability to reason about algorithms and graphics systems.


Regarding extra curriculars, I am the treasurer for the CMU Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers. Where I manage the organization's budget and facilitate event programming. Additionally, I am part of the CMU Game Creation Society where I have worked on game projects in teams throughout a semester. Furthermore, I play for "Magia Latina" at the CMU Intramural League every week.
